## Introduction

This section defines how data must be classified and handled across CARA COM MY systems.  
The goal is to ensure that sensitive data is stored, transmitted, and accessed appropriately to reduce the risk of leaks or misuse.

### ğŸš€ Data Transmission

When sending data, use the right channels to keep it safe.

<Steps>
    <Step title="Internal Communication">
        - Normal email (e.g., Gmail) and approved chat tools are acceptable for transmitting **Internal** data.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Secure Channels for Sensitive Data">
        When transmitting **Confidential** or **Restricted** data, secure and encrypted channels must be used.
        - **Email:** Use PGP/S/MIME encryption.
        - **APIs:**
            - **Public-facing APIs:** Must enforce HTTPS.
            - **Internal APIs:** Can use HTTP within a trusted network.
        - **Other Transmissions:** Use a secure protocol (e.g., SFTP).
    </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  If you are unsure which protocol to use for your specific use case, please contact the MIS team for guidance.
</Info>

## Summary

- **Classify your data:** Understand the difference between Public, Internal, Confidential, and Restricted data.
- **Handle with care:** Use the right tools and channels for storing and transmitting data.
- **When in doubt, ask:** Contact the MIS team if you're unsure about how to handle specific data.

<Warning>
  Data classification is not optional. All employees are expected to apply the
  correct classification when creating, storing, or transmitting information.
</Warning>
